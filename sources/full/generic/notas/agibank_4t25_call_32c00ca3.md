### Action Items

- [ ] Continue AI implementation across all C-level initiatives to become AI-driven company within 24 months 

- [ ] Ramp up private payroll credit portfolio growth in March 2026 

### INSS Relationship Update

- Agibank executed two operational agreements with INSS: one in November 2025 for benefits payments and another in January 2026 for payroll credit origination 

- Temporary suspensions impacted Q4 2025 credit origination but operations fully resumed by mid-January 2026 

- Origination pace returned to pre-suspension levels by end of February 2026 

- Company believes relationship issues are behind them and no further suspensions expected 

### Financial Results - Q4 2025

- Total revenue: R$ 2.96 billion, up 6% quarter-over-quarter 

- Net income: R$ 215 million 

- Active customers: 6.7 million, up 73% year-over-year 

- Total loan portfolio: R$ 34.9 billion, up 44% year-over-year 

- NPL ratio (90+ days): 3.7%, increased due to higher private payroll mix and INSS suspension impacts 

- Coverage ratio: 189.4% 

- Net interest margin: 12.5% for full year, impacted by shift toward secured loans and higher funding costs 

### Financial Results - Full Year 2025

- Total revenue: R$ 10.7 billion, up 46.8% year-over-year 

- Net interest income: R$ 4.7 billion, up 19% year-over-year 

- Net income: R$ 1.05 billion, up 31.8% year-over-year 

- Return on equity: 35.8% 

- Operating efficiency ratio: 40.6%, improved 590 basis points year-over-year 

- Total deposits: R$ 37.8 billion, up 50% from 2024 

- Capital adequacy ratio: 15.5%, estimated to reach ~19% with IPO proceeds 

### Credit Portfolio Breakdown

- Secured loans: 86% of portfolio (R$ 29.9 billion) 

- Unsecured loans: 14% of portfolio (R$ 4.9 billion) 

- Private payroll credit: R$ 0.9 billion (launched March 2025) 

- Public payroll credit: R$ 0.3 billion 

- INSS payroll credit market share: 8.9%, up 250 basis points from 2024 

### Strategic Positioning & Business Model

- Hybrid model combines digital banking with 1,100+ smart hubs nationwide to serve underbanked population 

- Target market: 100+ million social security beneficiaries and payroll workers 

- Total addressable market: R$ 2.1 trillion including payroll, credit cards, personal loans, insurance and fees 

- Customers with primary banking relationships average 5+ products, rising above 7 products for mature cohorts 

- Only offer unsecured credit to clients who receive salary with Agibank 

### Technology & AI Initiatives

- Company investing heavily to become AI-driven within 12-24 months 

- AI results already achieved: 40% cost reduction in call centers, AI agents 80% more efficient than human contact, 90% reduction in fraud alert review time 

- AI being used for lawsuits: AI agents read proceedings and prepare defenses, leading to provision model adjustment in Q4 

- Proprietary core banking system without legacy constraints enables integration with modern AI technology 

### Q4 Expense Dynamics

- SG&A decreased 31% quarter-over-quarter due to: (1) ~20% from lower variable costs to serve during INSS suspension, (2) ~80% from legal provision reversal after AI-improved lawsuit model 

- Commission and fee revenue declined in Q4 due to slowdown in production and cross-sell, but normalized by mid-quarter 

### Private Payroll Credit Strategy

- Launched in March 2025 with conservative approach to develop credit models 

- Beginning to ramp up production in March 2026 with more holistic growth approach 

- No cannibalization with existing products - represents complete upside 

- Company comfortable with developed credit models and sees large market opportunity 

### Competitive Positioning

- Three structural factors support long-term thesis: aging demographics, low education levels, low average income (65%+ population earns under $400/month) 

- Hybrid model creates barriers to replication: incumbents lack specialized setup, digital-only banks lack advisory channels for this segment 

- Company expects to maintain above-market origination share and continue growing market share 

### Forward Outlook

- Management comfortable with street consensus estimates for portfolio growth 

- NIM expected to remain at similar levels with continued mix shift toward secured loans, potential upside from SELIC rate decreases 

- Fee revenue from insurance expected to remain important and sustainable revenue stream 

- Company prioritizes long-term compounding returns over short-term outcomes
