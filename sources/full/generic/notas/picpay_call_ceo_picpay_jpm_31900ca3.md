# Call CEO Picpay - JPM

> [bloco não-suportado: transcription]



### Company Strategy & 2026 Priorities

- PicPay's primary objective for 2026 is to further penetrate the existing user base with credit products, which is vital for revenue growth, margin expansion, and increasing customer principality  

- The company reached 66 million clients in Q3 2025, with principality increasing from 27% to 32% over two years  

- PicPay uses a strict principality measurement: customers must bring more than 50% of their monthly income to the platform for consecutive months to be considered principal customers  

- Current customers generate R$65k in revenue per customer on average, but mature cohorts already deliver more than 2x that amount at R$145k, indicating significant growth potential as the base matures 

### Credit Growth Strategy: Focus on Secured Products

- Credit penetration remains low: PicPay's current user base represents 40% of the total market for credit cards and personal loans in Brazil, but the company only has 6% share of wallet  

- Five-year plan targets increasing share of wallet from 6% to 10-15% (approximately 12.5%) 

- By design, PicPay has a defensive credit approach with 75% of credit origination backed by secured products including secured credit cards, public/private payroll loans, and FGTS prepayment 

- Revenue mix is diversified: 50% comes from fees, commissions, and float on deposits; 50% from credit (33% unsecured, 17% secured) 

- Secured credit products are expected to grow faster than unsecured, maintaining a more diversified and defensive revenue mix compared to peers 

- Credit portfolio growth target remains 40-50% annually through 2027-28 to reach R$50 billion, with secured origination representing 70-75% of growth  

### Private Payroll Lending: Major Growth Opportunity

- Private payroll represents one of the largest captive markets still controlled by incumbent banks, currently around R$80 billion but expected to grow to R$400 billion  

- PicPay was the second company certified to operate private payroll in April 2025 and originated almost R$1 billion in the first 45 days  

- Recent monthly origination averaging around R$500 million 

- Initial NPL challenges: First payment cycle showed ~30% NPL, primarily due to operational issues with government database rather than credit risk 

- Current lifetime NPL expectation is around 30%, with recent vintages performing at high single digits  

- Distribution mix: 75% through PicPay's own app, 25% through government's CTPS platform 

- Currently underwriting with only direct payroll access; additional warrants (access to FGTS portion and severance packages) not yet implemented but will improve performance when available  

- Around 45-50% of PicPay's user base is formally employed, making this a highly relevant opportunity 

### Unsecured Credit: Progressive Limit Approach

- PicPay only offers unsecured credit to customers with existing transactional behavior on the platform 

- Implemented a "low and grow" progressive limit strategy similar to Nubank and Capital One to segregate good customers from bad over time 

- Strategy for 2026: Focus on increasing penetration and credit limits for best cohorts and good payers who have built credit behavior with PicPay  

- The progressive limit approach currently operates as a customer acquisition cost (CAC), not targeting optimal NPLs or margins initially 

- After 8-10 months, good payers are upgraded to standard credit limits with much better ROEs and NPL metrics 

### Financial Targets & Operating Efficiency

- Target ROE above 30% by 2027 with total capital ratio around 15% and common equity tier one around 11-11.5%  

- Q4 2025 already delivering ROEs above 20% 

- Cost-to-income ratio in Q4 close to low 50s, down more than 20 percentage points year over year 

- Company expects continued operating leverage with no inflection points anticipated 

- Key risk management metric: Loss absorption ratio targeting 50%, meaning expected credit losses could double and cohort would still break even  

- Coverage ratios are higher than peers: Stage 2 and 3 coverage above 60-65%, Stage 3 coverage above 80% 

### Technology & AI Investments

- PicPay began AI investments in 2023, launching first client-facing ChatGPT model in March 2023 

- Migrated all first-level customer service to AI approximately two years ago, avoiding hiring of around 2,500 new customer service representatives 

- Currently in second round of AI formulation models for credit underwriting, though not ready for mass rollout yet 

- No major hiring spikes expected; focus remains on scaling digital banking franchise in Brazil 

### Market Positioning & Competition

- 70% of Brazil's financial services profit pool remains with five incumbent banks, which are PicPay's primary target market   

- Over the last 24 months, top five sources of customer acquisition have consistently been the five largest incumbent banks 

- By design, PicPay targets incumbent banks rather than other digital players, as this is where the majority of profit pool resides 

- On average, Brazilians have five bank accounts, meaning PicPay overlaps with all major players including Nubank and Mercado Pago 

### Premium Product Launch: Epic

- Launched Epic premium tier in December 2025 with features including 200% CDI on deposits and enhanced cashback  

- Most popular feature: International purchases in three installments without interest, which no competitor offers 

- Strategy to move up the income pyramid is a long-term goal over many years, not expected to have huge short-term impact  

- Customer acquisition cost (CAC) currently around $4 and expected to increase due to competition and targeting more affluent customers 

### Business Diversification

- Currently 92% of gross profit comes from consumer banking, with 8% from SME banking and ecosystem/audiences business 

- Target: These two additional business areas should represent around 20% of gross profit in five years 

- SME banking showing strong growth: Monthly new business account onboarding increased from 4,000 at beginning of 2025 to 70-80,000 per month by year end 

- Strategic focus remains 100% on Brazil for at least the next 2-3 years, unlike competitors pursuing regional expansion 

### Credit Portability & Open Finance

- PicPay has invested significantly in Open Finance and is bullish on credit portability 

- However, does not expect portability to become materially relevant before 2027 due to ongoing operational and regulatory roadblocks  

### Wallet Disputes with Incumbent Banks

- Ongoing discussions with incumbent banks regarding facilitating credit through digital wallet (allowing third-party credit cards)  

- This business is becoming less relevant to PicPay: More than 50% of PIX finance transactions are now funded by PicPay's own cards (40% by volume) 

- PicPay's studies show no additional risk from using digital wallet for these transactions 

- Transaction type (cash-in) is allowed globally by payment schemes like Visa and MasterCard in markets including India and US 

- Discussion intensity has decreased compared to 1-2 years ago but could resurface 

### Macro Outlook

- Management is positive on Brazil macro for 2026  

- No changes observed in customer behavior; new tax exemption ceiling expected to provide positive tailwind by increasing disposable income 

- Asset quality remains stable with no signs of deterioration 

- Too early to definitively attribute positive data points to tax cut impact
