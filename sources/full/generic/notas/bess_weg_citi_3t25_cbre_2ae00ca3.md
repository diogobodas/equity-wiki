# BESS - WEG CITI 3T25 (CBRE)

> [bloco não-suportado: transcription]



### CBRI's Market Position

- CBRI has been operating in Brazil and Latin America for over 12 years, focusing on data center land acquisition and development 

- Since 2012, CBRI has acquired over 5.6 million square meters for data center operations in Latin America, representing 85%+ market share in real estate transactions 

- The Latin American data center market has grown from 200 megawatts in 2018 to nearly 1.2 gigawatts of installed capacity today 

### Impact of AI on Data Centers

- There was significant market reaction to DeepSeek's announcement about more efficient AI models, with concerns about reduced energy demands  

- Despite more efficient models, data centers will actually become more energy-dense, with power requirements increasing from 4-5 KVA per rack to 20+ KVA for AI workloads  

- Data production is growing exponentially (approaching 150 zettabytes per year, with projections of 2,500+ zettabytes by 2030-32) 

- This creates "Jevons paradox" - as processing becomes cheaper, more data will be processed, increasing overall demand 

### Brazil's Energy Infrastructure Challenges

- Brazil doesn't have an energy production problem; it has abundant renewable energy but faces transmission constraints 

- The primary bottleneck is transmission capacity, especially to high-demand areas like São Paulo 

- There's a misconception that AI data centers can be placed anywhere regardless of connectivity  

- São Paulo-Campinas axis remains the most connected region in Brazil, where 80% of data centers are currently located  

### Geographic Distribution Strategy

- Different data center workloads have different requirements:

- Financial and highly connected operations will remain in São Paulo 

- Data export and AI training centers can be located in more remote regions 

- Brazil needs to solve transmission issues within 6-12 months to capture the opportunity before global competitors 

### Brazil's Competitive Position

- Brazil has unique competitive advantages compared to global alternatives:

- 100% renewable energy matrix  

- Low energy costs (though transmission costs and fees add to this) 

- Land availability 

- Strategic geographic location with good connectivity to US East Coast and Europe 

- Main competitors include Australia, Middle East, India, Southeast Asia, Nordic countries, and Mexico  

- Areas where Brazil lags include approval speed, infrastructure investment pace, tax issues, and regulatory environment  

### Real Estate Players in the Data Center Market

- Traditional players like Equinix and Digital Realty are now seeing competition from real estate companies like Prologis and Goodman  

- These new entrants are exploring different positions in the value chain, primarily focusing on "powered land" opportunities  

- Powered land involves providing land plus energy solution (permits and/or physical infrastructure)  

- Some companies like GLP have chosen to compete directly as operators, while others like Prologis are staying more as infrastructure providers 

### Energy Solutions and Battery Technology

- Brazil has a 100% integrated national energy system, allowing a data center in São Paulo to use energy from wind farms in distant regions 

- Battery technology is more promising for grid stabilization than as backup power for data centers  

- Traditional diesel generators remain the trusted backup solution for data centers due to reliability and proven performance  

### Regulatory Environment

- Redata (tax incentive program) is critical, with the report needing to be signed by January 17, 2024  

- 2026 is seen as the window of opportunity for investments from American companies 

- Brazil's data protection agency has been elevated from an authority to an agency, signaling more government focus on this area 

- Upcoming regulatory discussions will focus on AI, data sovereignty, and related topics  

- There are emerging concerns about data centers being seen as "neocolonial" extractive industries using Brazilian resources
