# Nu - US Bofa

> [bloco não-suportado: transcription]



### Meeting Overview

- Expert discussion on Nubank's potential entry into the US banking market

- Analysis of competitive landscape, regulatory environment, and strategic opportunities

- Participants included banking analysts with expertise in neobanks, traditional banks, and fintech companies

### SoFi's Success Strategy

- Unique among neobanks by targeting high-earning individuals rather than low-income/unbanked population

- Used "HENRY" strategy (High Earning, Not Rich Yet) - focused on graduate students from elite universities

- Started with student loan refinancing, then expanded to high-yield savings accounts requiring direct deposit

- Early mover in offering integrated app with crypto, banking, and investing (before obtaining bank license)

- Differentiation came from strategic customer segmentation and higher interest rates on savings accounts

### US Deposit Market Challenges

- Online banking has existed for 20+ years in the US, but no clear "low-cost online deposit" model exists

- Physical branches remain important competitive advantage - banks continuing branch expansion strategies

- Branches serve as local market billboards and are top consideration factor for consumers when selecting banks

- Digital banking differentiation has not been compelling enough to drive customer switching

- Online deposits are inherently high-cost (require competitive interest rates to attract customers)

### Regulatory Environment

- Current administration may process bank license applications more quickly than previous years

- Faster processing doesn't mean easier approval - still rigorous requirements

- Similar trend seen with crypto firms filing for trust charters

- Foreign bank entry faces standard regulatory scrutiny but current environment may be more favorable

### US Banking Competition Dynamics

- Extremely competitive and fragmented market with 4,000+ small banks

- Each business segment has best-in-class specialized competitors (e.g., Capital One/Amex for cards, specialized lenders)

- No bank has achieved dominance across all segments

- Banks consistently compete on multiple fronts: traditional banks, fintechs, big tech companies

- Market structure makes it difficult for new entrants to gain significant scale

### Capital One Case Study

- Top credit card issuer (now largest after Discover acquisition) but struggles in deposit gathering relative to card success

- Has zero-cost deposits only because of physical branch networks (acquired ING/HSBC businesses, plus regional branches)

- Online deposits are high-cost savings accounts

- Continues opening "cafe" branches - indicates physical presence matters for deposit gathering

- Discover acquisition may provide interchange arbitrage opportunity (uncapped network fees vs. capped traditional interchange)

### AI and Technology in Lending

- Upstart uses AI underwriting but model underperformed during 2022-2023 stress period

- True AI underwriting faces regulatory challenges (must provide rejection reasons)

- Most companies use machine learning, not generative AI

- Traditional banks (Capital One, Amex) already use sophisticated machine learning models

- Competitive advantage comes more from consumer experience/conversion than backend underwriting technology

- Newer companies differentiate through easier application processes (e.g., Affirm's 5-6 data points)

### Small Business Banking Opportunities

- SMEs prefer local branch presence for banking relationships

- Regional banks and specialized players (e.g., Live Oak Bank) already serve digital-savvy small businesses

- Corporate payment space has strong competitors (Ramp, Brex, Amex)

- Transaction account relationships are key - companies with checking accounts have stronger competitive position

- Value proposition would need to be clearly differentiated from existing options

### Underserved Market Segments

- Low-income population is poorly served by both traditional banks and fintechs

- Some success with targeted approaches (e.g., East West Bank serving Chinese American community for 30+ years)

- Debit card interchange regulations created opportunity for small online banks serving lower-income customers

- Cash App and similar services serve specific niches

- Latin American population in US could be target market, though business case unclear

### Credit Card Market Structure

- Rewards programs are key competitive dimension, especially in super-prime segment

- Lower-income segment competition centers on cashback, credit building products

- Subprime cards face high loss rates (mid-to-high teens) making profitability challenging

- Industry trend toward focusing on prime/super-prime customers due to risk-return profile

- SoFi has struggled with credit card product despite success in other areas

### Profitability Benchmarks

- Largest US banks achieving 12-14% ROE currently

- JPMorgan best-in-class at 16-17% ROE (highest in 20 years)

- Higher interest rate environment benefits deposit-rich banks

- Banks have fixed-rate assets repricing from ~2% to 4-5%, improving margins

- Expected steady state around 13-15% ROE absent credit cycle or rate collapse

### Key Challenges for Nubank US Entry

- Unclear value proposition and target customer segment

- No obvious underserved market comparable to Brazil/Mexico situations

- US consumers already have extensive digital banking options

- Physical presence appears important for deposit gathering at scale

- High competition across all banking products and segments

- Technology advantage alone has not proven sufficient for disruption

- Cross-border services for Latin American population possible angle but business case uncertain

### Outstanding Questions

- What specific pain points will Nubank address in US market?

- What will be the primary revenue driver (checking, credit cards, SME, other)?

- How will deposit gathering strategy work without physical presence?

- What differentiation beyond technology will attract customers?

- Timeline for regulatory approval uncertain but potentially faster under current administration
