### Meeting Context

- J.P. Morgan client call with Porto executives on earnings day to discuss medium-term strategic priorities  

- Focus on 2026 priorities, competitive landscape, and growth opportunities across business segments  

### 2026 Strategic Priorities

- Budget for 2026 already approved with three main strategic drivers 

- Cross-selling strategy has been fruitful, with opportunities to accelerate across four verticals 

- Credit cards as major payment method for all services and products

- Embedded insurance services

- Additional services from Porto Saúde (health division) 

- Improved penetration among insurance brokers

- 45,000 active brokers out of approximately 65,000 in Brazil

- Expanded product portfolio reaching new specialist brokers (e.g., health segment)  

- Brand segmentation strategy to protect against price wars

- Multiple brands covering different market segments: Azul (entry-level), Mitsui, Itaú, Porto, Porto Premium, and Porto Private 

- New motorcycle insurance products introduced in a growing segment 

### Auto Insurance Competitive Landscape

- Price war already underway with cyclical competitive dynamics  

- Pattern: one player becomes aggressive, others lose market share, then react by cutting prices  

- Cycle began Q3 2023 with one aggressive player, followed by reactions from competitors 

- Porto's strategy as market leader:

- Avoiding participation in price war to prevent industry-wide margin deterioration 

- Focusing on incentives, marketing, brand positioning, and broker activation 

- Comfortable with market share ranging between 26-28%  

- Current industry environment has shown longer stability with higher ROEs 

- Competitor analysis:

- Allianz currently most aggressive in pricing 

- Tokyo Marine grew from 5% to 14-15% market share over 10 years 

- Tokyo's strength is superior digital customer experience, though Porto has worked to close this gap  

- Market share "donors" include Allianz, HDI and Bradesco 

### Healthcare Business Strategy

- "Health tech" asset-light model showing strong growth 

- Current status:

- Grown from 200,000 to 815,000 lives in the system 

- 1.5-5% market share in key regions (São Paulo, Rio de Janeiro, Brasília) 

- Growth outlook:

- Expected to reach 1 million lives sooner than anticipated 

- Significant room for growth in existing markets with currently low penetration 

- Competitive advantages:

- Asset-light model versus vertically integrated competitors 

- End-to-end process control with low claims rates and G&A costs 

- Low fraud rates and high-quality medicine in a high-performance network  

- ROEs above 30% and growing at 20-25% annually 

- Conservative underwriting approach 

### Banking Services Growth

- Digital banking services growing without major promotion

- 800,000 digital bank accounts with 250,000 daily active users 

- Strong consortium product performance

- Growing market with increased industry advertising raising overall awareness  

- Positioned as solution for customers to purchase expensive assets without down payment 

- Credit offerings

- Car equity and home equity loans for liability management 

- Conservative approach with maximum 70% loan-to-value ratio
