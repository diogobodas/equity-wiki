# Nubank Call 4T25

> [bloco não-suportado: transcription]



### Key Highlights

- Nu ended 2025 with 131 million customers, adding 17 million net new customers with an 83% activity rate 

- Revenue reached $4.9 billion in Q4, up 45% year-over-year 

- Net income hit $895 million with a record 33% ROE  

- Efficiency ratio fell below 20% for the first time (19.9%) under new methodology 

- Revenue per active customer (RPAC) reached $15, up 27% year-over-year 

- Introduced new managerial P&L framework for enhanced transparency and comparability  

### New Managerial Reporting Framework

- Nu introduced a managerial P&L to provide clearer view of value creation across multi-product, multi-segment, multi-country platform 

- Framework is derived entirely from IFRS results through structural reorganization of line items 

- Preserves net income, cash flow, equity and regulatory capital 

- Historical data updated back to Q1 2021 under new framework 

- Full reconciliation to IFRS available in managerial P&L reconciliation report on IR website 

### Financial Performance

Portfolio Growth

- Total portfolio reached $32.7 billion, up 40% year-over-year 

- Credit cards grew 12.2% quarter-over-quarter, strongest growth since end of 2023 

- Unsecured lending surpassed $8 billion with record $4 billion in originations 

- Secured lending grew 3.8% quarter-over-quarter, impacted by FGTS regulation changes that reduced originations by 50-60%  

- Unused credit limits increased from $18 billion to $29 billion (60% increase), driven by AI-powered credit underwriting  

Deposits & Funding

- Total deposits reached $41.9 billion, up 29% year-over-year 

- Cost of deposits declined to 87% of intrabank rate on consolidated basis 

- Growth reflected typical Q4 seasonality in Brazil (13th salary) 

- Mexico deposits resumed growth after Q3 pricing adjustments 

Profitability Metrics

- Gross profit reached nearly $2 billion, up 38% year-over-year  

- Risk-adjusted NIM closed at 10.5%, would have been stable excluding one-time $25 million Mexico PROSOFIPO contribution  

- Float contribution increased from strong deposit inflows and improved funding economics 

- Credit card market share in Brazil increased by 50 basis points, largest gain in over 10 quarters  

Asset Quality

- 15-90 day NPLs improved for fourth consecutive quarter, declining 20 bps to 4.1% 

- 90+ NPLs declined 10 bps to 6.6% 

- Asset quality tracking expectations with no signs of deterioration  

- Coverage ratios remain strong on both total balances and 90+ NPLs 

- Typical seasonal uptick in 15-90 day NPLs expected in Q1 2026 

- Now presenting consolidated NPL metrics across all countries given portfolio diversification 

Credit Loss Allowances

- CLA increase entirely driven by portfolio growth and increased unused credit limits, not asset quality deterioration  

- CLA to average credit portfolio ratio at 3.9%, expected to trend toward or below average of 3.3-3.9% in coming quarters 

Tax & Non-Recurring Items

- Effective tax rate benefited from $58 million DTA remeasurement following CSLL rate increase in Brazil   

- $22 million provision expense for return to office transition  

- $25 million one-time PROSOFIPO contribution in Mexico (sector-wide deposit insurance fund)   

Capital & Liquidity

- Total capital stands at $8.9 billion at holdings level 

- $3.6 billion covers regulatory requirements across three geographies 

- $2.2 billion represents excess capital in operating entities 

- $3 billion sits at Nu Holdings as unrestricted cash available for growth and global expansion 

- Available funding of $38.8 billion represents approximately 2x net credit portfolio of $19 billion 

### 2025 Strategic Achievements

Geographic Expansion

- Brazil: Became largest private financial institution by customer count with 113 million customers and 86% activity rate 

- Mexico: Reached 14 million customers, roughly half received their first credit card through Nu 

- Colombia: Surpassed 4 million customers, subscription-based credit card significantly increased approval rates 

Product Innovation

- Launched over 100 new products and features across markets 

- Payments: Evolved PIX with AI features, launched instant payments in Colombia, expanded Mexico cash-in/cash-out network to 30,000+ points 

- Credit: Expanded payroll loan modalities in Brazil, launched subscription credit card in Colombia, introduced Fresh Start program and under-18 credit card 

- SME: Scaled credit products and launched charging assistant for cash flow management 

- Digital ecosystem reached 12+ million unique active customers across Nu Sell, Nu Pay, and Nu Travel 

AI Development

- Foundation model NuFormer now in production for credit decisioning in Brazil 

- AI improving underwriting, conversion, and service quality 

- PIX with AI surpassing 10 million monthly active users 

US Expansion

- Received conditional approval from OCC for U.S. National Bank Charter in January 

### 2026 Priorities

Three Strategic Pillars 

1. Winning in Core Markets

- Brazil and Mexico will absorb majority of capital and management attention 

- Brazil focus: Deepen mass market leadership, expand share of wallet and ARPAC, strengthen SMB, grow high-income presence through Ultravioleta 

- Mexico: Finalizing banking license process critical to unlock next phase of credit growth 

- Colombia: Continue scaling credit and launching new products 

- Focus remains on experience, principality, and monetization across all markets 

1. Strengthen Foundations for International Expansion

- Build operational groundwork for U.S. opportunity based on conditional bank charter 

- Latin America remains primary growth engine 

1. AI as a Superpower

- Expand NuFormer to lending in Brazil and credit cards in Mexico 

- Put AI directly into customers' hands 

- Move toward vision of AI-powered personal banker in every customer's pocket 

Investment Year Expectations

- 2026 characterized as inflection year transitioning from Latin American leader to global digital banking platform 

- Efficiency ratio may face upward pressure in near term (next 4-6 quarters) from deliberate investments 

- Three investment categories: Return to office (80-100 bps impact on efficiency ratio), AI and new technologies (talent, R&D, GPUs), globalization foundations   

- Structural drivers of operating leverage remain unchanged, efficiency expected to improve over medium term 

### Q&A Session Highlights

AI and Disruption

- AI represents both challenge and opportunity, with net-net more opportunity for Nu 

- Credit revenue most sustainable in financial services due to capital intensity, regulatory nature, balance sheet requirements, and proprietary data 

- RPAC currently $15 with significant opportunity to reach competitor levels around $40 through AI-enabled cross-sell 

- AI improving revenue through better underwriting and cross-sell, plus cost benefits across all functions  

- 95% of global financial services profits still concentrated in incumbent banks with higher cost structures 

Credit Growth Drivers

- Credit limit increases deployed successfully using predictive AI, with unused limits growing $11 billion (60%)  

- Market share in purchase volume in Brazil increased 50 bps in Q4, biggest gain in 10+ quarters 

- Credit limit increases to continue unfolding through 2026-2027 and expand to Mexico, Colombia, and other use cases  

- Without FGTS headwind, portfolio growth would have been 13-14% quarter-over-quarter vs. reported 11% 

Secured Lending Strategy

- FGTS originations down 50-60% post-November 1st regulations, but public payroll (consignado) growth accelerating  

- Bullish on public payroll given lower costs, improving portability, and declining interest rate environment 

- Private payroll represents massive structural opportunity but waiting for collateral mechanisms to mature  

- Not yet seeing cannibalization or structural subordination risks from private payroll in customer base 

High-Income and Affluent Segments

- High-income segment (12,000+ reais/month) growing 40% year-over-year 

- Super core segment (5,000-12,000 reais/month) growing at 100% in 2025, fastest-growing segment  

- 40% of Brazilians in high-income bracket already Nu customers, but with low share of wallet 

- Improvements needed in credit limits, value proposition (cashback, travel integration, frequent flyer program), and investment platform   

- Investment platform approaching product parity with fixed income, equity, crypto, and visualization tools 

US Expansion Plans

- Targeting specific niches and sub-segments that are similar in size to Brazil 

- Strategy will be very targeted and disciplined, not "shooting in all directions" 

- Initial investments in 2026 focused on team building and product, characterized as de minimis 

- Marketing partnerships announced but US not expected to be significant investment source in 2026 

Operating Expenses

- Q4 marketing spike reflects typical seasonal pattern 

- Increases in other expenses driven by tax breaks related to technology investments 

- Technology investment tax breaks more than offset by effective tax rate benefits
