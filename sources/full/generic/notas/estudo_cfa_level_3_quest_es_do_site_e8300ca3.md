The process of setting capital market expectations (CMEs) involves the following seven steps:

1. Specify the set of expectations needed, including the time horizon(s) to which they apply.

2. Research the historical record.

3. Specify the method(s) and/or model(s) to be used and their information requirements.

4. Determine the best sources for information needs.

5. Interpret the current investment environment using the selected data and methods, applying experience and judgment.

6. Provide the set of expectations needed, documenting conclusions.

7. Monitor actual outcomes and compare them with expectations, providing feedback to improve the expectation-setting process.

### Reasons for Measurement Error in Passive Immunization

1. Estimation of Interest Rates:

- Interest rates, which are crucial in calculating BPV, are often estimated using yield curves. Errors in estimating these curves can lead to measurement errors.

1. Modeling Assumptions:

- The models used to calculate BPV assume certain market conditions and behaviors which may not hold true in reality. Deviations from these assumptions can introduce errors.

1. Reinvestment Risk:

- Even with set cash flow amounts and dates, reinvestment of coupon payments can pose a risk if the actual reinvestment rates differ from the assumed rates used in the BPV calculation.

1. Changes in Yield Curve Shape:

- The BPV is sensitive to changes in the shape of the yield curve. Any unanticipated shifts can affect the accuracy of BPV measurement.
