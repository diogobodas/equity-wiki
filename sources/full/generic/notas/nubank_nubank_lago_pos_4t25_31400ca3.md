# Nubank - Lago Pos 4T25

> [bloco não-suportado: transcription]



### Key Strategic Initiatives & Investment Areas

Three Primary Investment Headwinds for 2026  

- Return to Office (RTO): Starting July 1, 2026, requiring employees in office at least two times per week, with impact of approximately 80-100 basis points on efficiency ratio (closer to 80bp in modeled scenarios)  

- One-off investments in office refurbishing

- Ongoing costs for leases, transportation, and relocation

- Expected to improve coordination and innovation after 3.5+ years of remote work 

- Internationalization: Investments in US expansion and other markets creating timing mismatch between expenses (2026) and revenue generation (2027 and beyond) 

- AI Investments: R&D initiatives focused primarily on predictive AI for credit underwriting, with investments expected to be lower than RTO levels   

### Operating Leverage & Expense Outlook

Core Business Performance  

- Core business (Brazil, Mexico, Colombia) expected to continue delivering healthy operational leverage with expenses growing slower than revenues

- No specific guidance provided on overall efficiency ratio trajectory for 2026  

- Advised investors to view efficiency ratio changes alongside effective tax rate (ETR) improvements for complete picture 

Tax Rate Optimization Strategy   

- Multiple levers for ETR reduction across geographies:

- Technology investment programs allowing 1:1 offset of corporate income tax (Brazil, Mexico)

- Tax-exempt treasury investments (similar to US muni bonds)

- JSP on capital payments to holding company (Brazil)

- Expected structural reductions in ETR over next 8-12 quarters (excluding one-time Q4 DTA revaluation)  

- Tax benefits may partially or fully offset incremental OPEX investments  

### Credit Loss Allowance & Provisioning

Unused Credit Limits Impact   

- Unused credit limits grew from ~$17B to ~$28B (~$11B increase, 60%+ growth)

- CLA builds provisions upfront for both credit assets and unused limits

- Provisions are fully reserved for limits already granted, including expected translation to interest-bearing balances (IBB)  

Credit Limit Expansion Timeline & Strategy  

- Typical cycle: 18-24 months from credit limit increase to full IBB capture (80% within 12-18 months)

- Market share of purchase volume (PV) in Brazil grew 52-53 basis points in Q4 2025—fastest gain in 10-11 quarters 

- Ratio of unused to used limits expected to converge to historical average of 1.1-1.2x 

Credit Quality & Asset Performance  

- No signs of asset quality deterioration across Brazil, Mexico, or Colombia

- Coverage ratios flat to improving, indicating stable to improving credit mix

- Increased CLA driven entirely by credit book growth, not deterioration concerns

Potential Tailwinds  

- Brazil tax reforms (effective January 1, 2026) will increase disposable income for individuals earning up to 7,400 reais—representing large portion of customer base

- Benefits not yet incorporated into provisioning models, could provide positive surprise

### AI Strategy & Applications

Revenue Enhancement Focus  

- Prioritizing revenue opportunities over cost optimization given RPAC/ARPU gap ($15 vs incumbents' $45)

- Key revenue use cases:

- AI Private Banker (AIPB): Delivering private banking-level service to broader population using AI, open banking, and real-time payments  

- Credit Underwriting: Neural networks and predictive AI significantly improving underwriting capabilities beyond previous model efficiency saturation  

Credit Model Deployment Roadmap   

- Currently deployed: First generation of predictive AI on credit limit increase policy (CLIP) for credit cards in Brazil

- Pipeline includes seven generations of improvements across:

- Additional CLIP generations

- Acquisition models (initial customer approval)

- Lending products

- Mexico and Colombia markets

- Velocity of credit underwriting improvements has "experienced a shift in its derivative" 

Cost Optimization Opportunities    

- Customer experience and operations improvements underway

- Broader productivity gains expected across finance functions (controllership, FP&A, tax, AML)

- Significant opportunity in deposit pricing optimization—cost of funding is second-largest expense after credit losses

- Potential 5-10% reduction in all-in cost of funding through AI-driven sophistication  

Technology Approach  

- Partnering with hyperscalers (AWS, GCP, exploring Microsoft Azure)

- "Fast adopter" strategy—not building LLM models or competing with hyperscalers on hardware

- Focused R&D investment at intersection of retail financial services and AI

- Investments structured with gates, unlocking additional spend only after building NPV-positive conviction 

### Geographic Expansion

US Market Approach  

- Banking license: Provisional OCC approval received, awaiting Fed approval (expected within 12-18 months based on historical timelines)

- Product offering: Full digital banking experience across all states covering payments, savings, and credit  

- Go-to-market details expected second half of 2026

- Organic growth strategy—no plans for major fintech/banking acquisitions (small acquihires possible)  

US Investment Philosophy   

- Positioned as "low-cost transformational option"

- Ring-fencing resources to protect Latin America execution quality

- Small team in Miami (~20 people) for customer research, product development, regulatory discussions 

- Will only scale after achieving product-market fit and positive unit economics  

- Willing to cut losses and redeploy capital if hypotheses prove untrue 

Market Context & Challenges  

- US CAC typically $150-$1,000 vs Nubank's $4-5 in Latin America 

- Core LatAm markets running at ~$3B earnings power—remain primary focus 

- Opportunity: States like Florida, Texas, California each have GDP exceeding Brazil 

Mexico Operations    

- Already profitable under local GAAP by end of 2025 (not optimizing for this metric)

- Credit assets growing 50-60% annually (both credit cards and lending)

- Lending showing stronger than expected growth—may become more relevant than credit cards

- Cost of funding optimized down ~26%

- Headcount associated with Mexico operations grew only 16-18% in 2025

- Banking license approval process in progress—will unlock payrolls, PMS, and new segments  

### Credit Products & Innovation

Payroll Lending Strategy  

- Private payroll lending enables access to employment data and salary collateral previously controlled by incumbent banks

- Early performance challenges: First payment defaults in double digits—collateral mechanisms not yet functioning as expected  

- Hypothesis: Incumbents may be converting existing relationships; Nubank facing higher-risk applicants (>50% with negative credit bureau records) 

- Optimistic medium-term outlook drawing parallels to public payroll loan evolution (took 12-18 months to mature) 

Credit Limit Expansion Details  

- Utilization rates vary significantly by segment:

- Mass market: Higher utilization of credit limit increases

- High-income customers: Lower percentage utilization on $100K+ limits

- 2026-2027 expansion magnitude expected to potentially match 2025 levels 

- Expansion not linear but velocity of improvements has accelerated
