### Key Financial Performance

- Record net income of 336 million reais with ROE of 14.2%, marking a significant milestone in Inter's profitability journey 

- Net revenue reached 2.1 billion reais, up 29% year-on-year and 8% sequentially, driven primarily by growth in the credit book 

- Efficiency ratio improved from 47.1% to 45.2%, demonstrating strong operating leverage of Inter's digital banking model 

- Assets to equity ratio increased from 7.9x to 9.4x year-on-year, showing optimization of capital structure 

- Total funding reached 68 billion reais, growing 35% year-on-year, with strong performance in both time deposits and transactional accounts 

- Low cost of funding remains a key competitive advantage at 68.2% of CDI (or 65.1% using fixed business days) 

### Loan Portfolio Performance

- Loan book grew 30% year-on-year and 9% quarter-on-quarter (36% annualized) 

- Private payroll loans emerged as a significant growth driver, reaching 1.3 billion reais with over 300,000 clients 

- Inter outpaced the market in key portfolios:

- Private payroll and personal loans grew 37% year-on-year to 9 billion reais 

- Home equity grew 33% year-on-year (market grew 21%), positioning Inter as the #2 player in originations  

- Credit cards grew 30% while maintaining conservative risk approach 

- Credit card portfolio successfully being reshaped with interest-earning portfolio (IEP) now representing over 23% of the total, up from 20% last year 

### Asset Quality

- 15-90 day NPL ratio remained stable at 4.1%, while 90-day past due metric improved to 4.5% (10bps improvement) 

- Cost of risk increased to 5.35%, primarily driven by upfront provisioning for the new private payroll portfolio 

- NPL formation and Stage 3 formation at 1.65% and 1.46% respectively, in line with historical trends 

- Private payroll loans expected to converge to high single-digit delinquency levels long-term, better than initially forecasted 

### Market Share Progress

- Home equity for individuals reached 8.9% market share in portfolio balance, making Inter the second-largest underwriter in Brazil 

- FX transactions reached 8.4% market share, driven by high engagement in the global account product 

- Inter holds approximately 10% market share in private payroll loan originations, though overall portfolio share is around 2.1% 

- Management highlighted potential of private payroll market to reach 250-300 billion reais (currently around 90 billion) 

### Strategic Initiatives

- Focus on continuous innovation through seven vertical business lines that work interconnectedly to enhance client value and profitability 

- Credit card reshaping strategy progressing through PICS financing, monthly limit reassessments, and new installment plan offerings 

- Preparing for upcoming Centralized Invoice Discounting Clearance House (Duplicatas Escriturais) to accelerate SMB lending in early 2026  

- Client monetization improving with NetRPAC reaching 33.2 reais, while mature clients generate close to 90 reais 

### Outlook and Long-term Goals

- Management remains committed to the "60-30-30" plan (6 million clients, 30% efficiency ratio, 30% ROE) 

- Current ROE has improved from 0% to almost 15%  

- High interest rates (SELIC) in Brazil identified as the main headwind to achieving 30% ROE target  

- Net interest margins expected to continue expanding for at least the next four quarters 

- Fee income projected to grow around 20% going forward
