# Acquired - TSMC

Transistor - It's a switch, either something will pass or not.

Integrated Circuit - Before the IC you would have to wire switches individually. The IC yoy can put lots of switches into a silicon.

There is so much IP, making that stuff is so hard.

How did Morris have the idea?

When TI made new chips with their fabs, they charged a lot. Because fixed costs are high, and starting yields are low, there is a learning curve.

His idea was to start at full capacity, to accelerate the learning curve and not to have to charge so much. Charging so much makes demand weaker and in turn makes the learning curve slower because of slower starting production.

Learning curve pricing - To reduce prices when you didn't have to.

Taiwan was only good at manufacturing

Average gross margin of a taiwan company in 1980 was 4%-5%.

He went to a research park in Taiwan, invited by a minister there.

Offer he could not refuse, go start a business.

Is not just plain manufacturing, it has a LOT of IP.

At first, they only outrsourced when they didnt have spare capacity. And the chips that didn't make money, they outsourced to TSMC.
