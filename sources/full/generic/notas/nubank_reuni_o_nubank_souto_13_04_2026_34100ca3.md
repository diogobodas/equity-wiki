# Reunião Nubank Souto - 13-04-2026

> [bloco não-suportado: transcription]



### Overview

Henrique hosted an investor Q&A call with Arthur and João to address three main concerns from foreign investors: NPL trends in Brazil, Nubank US expansion strategy, and Mexico operations.  The call covered operational updates across all three markets and fielded questions from investors about strategy, competition, and regulatory developments.

### NPL Trends in Brazil

- Foreign investor concerns: Investors have expressed concern about rising NPL trends in the Brazilian financial system, particularly given that 98% of Nubank's loan book consists of unsecured personal loans  

- Nubank's underwriting approach: Credit decisions are made to allow navigation through cycles, with a resilience buffer in expected losses embedded in net present value calculations that enable positive returns even with deteriorating losses 

- Current NPL status: The team emphasized seasonality effects in Q1 (customers overspend during Black Friday and holidays, with recurring expenses like school tuition in Q1) 

- Q4 2024 behavior: Higher growth in total book with movement toward more risk spectrum as secured lending was lower in Q4; this behavior might continue in Q1 2025 

- No signs of deterioration: At the current customer base, there are no signs of deterioration yet beyond normal seasonality 

- Credit limit increases: The process of updating models and adjusting limits is ongoing and recurring, not done in "waves" - decisions are based on transactionality data rather than macro inputs  

- FGTS impact: FGTS credit book is still present despite decreasing originations starting November 2024, as these loans have longer duration - the effect will be gradual 

- Mix effects: Potential positive mix effect from increasing public payroll loan originations could offset FGTS decreases  

- Desenrola program: The 2023 program was slightly NPV positive for customers who adopted it but didn't move the needle significantly  

- Desenrola 2.0 outlook: The potential new program could be marginally positive - more valuable as a better alternative to other proposed regulations like interest caps than for the program itself   

### Nubank US Expansion

- Marketing vs. product timing: The naming rights sponsorship of the new Miami stadium and Inter Miami sponsorship are part of building brand equity and trust in the US, which takes significant time  

- Multi-market brand value: These sponsorships (Inter Miami, F1/Mercedes) serve both US brand building and global strategy - Brazil is the largest F1 audience globally, and soccer resonates strongly in Brazil and Mexico  

- Product strategy: High-level strategy involves offering a bank account and credit card product to US citizens - similar playbook to Brazil, Mexico, and Colombia with no-fee credit card and compelling bank account value proposition 

- Primary banking relationship goal: The objective is to make customers use Nubank as their primary bank, not a shallow relationship - this requires extensive foundational work 

- Regulatory progress: Received conditional approval from OCC in 121 days (faster than expected), with final approval timeline now estimated at 18 months  

- 2026 expectations: 2026 will provide better clarity on products and strategy in the US, with potential to launch initial products without waiting for final OCC approval  

- Competitive differentiation vs. Chime: Specific differentiation strategy is not being disclosed yet, with a louder announcement planned in coming months 

- Customer acquisition costs: Not being disclosed at this time 

### Mexico Operations

- Profitability progress: Significant margin improvements achieved through adjusting deposit yields (reducing cost of funding) and accelerating credit portfolio growth 

- Breakeven timing: No specific guidance on breakeven point as the team may accelerate growth if opportunities arise, potentially postponing breakeven to maximize long-term returns 

- NPL volatility: Regulatory data showed volatility in February (for January NPLs) due to technical/mechanical changes in reporting (90+ days vs. 60+ days), but the team is comfortable with current NPL levels 

- Competition landscape: Primary competition is with cash (digitalizing the Mexican population), not incumbents or digital players  

- BBVA dynamics: BBVA is the main incumbent, well-run but focused on corporates and high-income segments rather than broader population credit expansion 

- Mercado Pago: Viewed as respectable competition that could help digitalize the Mexican population in a mutually beneficial way 

- Revolut: Very different business model - lacks the credit underwriting expertise Nubank has built over 14 years in Brazil and 5-6 years in Mexico  

- Credit as competitive advantage: Offering credit is essential to making Nubank the primary banking relationship for Mexican customers 

- Interchange rate caps: No updates on credit card interchange rate regulation - positive news as the industry (banks, fintechs, acquirers) successfully demonstrated that strict regulation would block market growth  

### Banking Licenses

- Brazil: Must define plan with Central Bank within approximately one month, then 12 months to implement 

- Brazil rationale: Required only because Central Bank mandated that financial players using "bank" or "banco" in their brand must have a banking license - won't affect customers or products 

- Mexico: In the middle of final audit from CNBV and Banxico, with 50% of local team directly involved 

- Mexico timeline: Expected completion in a few months, which will free up 50% of team productivity currently focused on authorization 

- US: Already covered under US expansion section above

### Colombia Market

- Subscription card product: New product with embedded fees that allows better NPV despite interest caps, enabling movement down the income pyramid 

- Approval rate improvement: Approval rate increased from 9% to 42% with the subscription card 

- Growth opportunity: Product unlocked customer eligibility and balance sheet growth in Colombia  

### Return to Office Strategy

- Strategic rationale: Bold decision to bring 10,000 employees back to office to improve collaboration, innovation, and engagement, translating to productivity improvements 

- Phased implementation: Starting July 2025 with designated days (not employee choice initially) due to limited space 

- Full implementation: By January 2027, all offices ready with hybrid-first model requiring three days per week in office 

- Cost structure: New recurring expense line for rentals and infrastructure, with $25 million one-off cost already provisioned in Q4 for mobility packages and office setup 

- Expense dilution: While recurring, office costs will be dilutive over time as revenues and portfolio grow much faster than office-related expenses  

### Credit Strategy and Portfolio Mix

- Risk-adjusted NPV framework: Portfolio growth decisions based on risk-adjusted NPV lens rather than minimizing cost of credit or CLA 

- Stress testing: New credit lines require positive marginal NPV even if expected credit loss doubles 

- FGTS restrictions: Government imposed restrictions in November on number of installments and refinancing, causing 50%+ drop in new originations  

- Mix effect on yields: Despite higher income segment growth, mass market remains the healthier and larger growth driver - combined with FGTS decline, mix effect may maintain or improve yields   

### Public Payroll Loans

- Agreement coverage: Currently have close to 75% of total market availability in terms of agreements, with 25% still to capture 

- Agreement structure: Each public entity requires separate agreements (São Paulo State, São Paulo City, Rio de Janeiro, federal via SIAPE, etc.) - different from private consignado which only requires Dataprev integration  

- Growth driver: Main growth driver is gaining traction within existing agreements rather than adding new agreements 

- Positive seasonality: First half of year sees positive effects from adjustments to retirement benefits and public employee salaries  

### Long-term Growth Strategy

- Multi-geography approach: Brazil printing $3 billion net income after scaling since 2019; Mexico launched March 2020 and now approaching breakeven; US planting seeds similar to Mexico six years ago  

- Timeline perspective: Takes 6-7 years to scale credit business in new geography - this long-term approach drives sustained growth
