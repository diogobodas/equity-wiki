### Financial Highlights

- Record quarterly net income of $783 million with ROE of 31%, up 39% year-over-year on an FX-neutral basis  

- Gross profit reached $1.8 billion, up 32% year-over-year, with gross profit margin expanding to 43.5% 

- Efficiency ratio decreased slightly to 27.7%, reflecting continued progress in productivity and operating leverage 

- Net interest income reached $2.3 billion, up 32% year-over-year, driven by expansion of the credit portfolio 

- Risk-adjusted net interest margins expanded to 9.9% in the quarter, underscoring portfolio resilience and quality 

### Credit Portfolio Performance

- Total credit portfolio balances reached $30.4 billion in Q3, up 42% year-over-year on an FX-neutral basis 

- Credit cards accelerated during the quarter, supported by enhanced credit model precision 

- Secured lending grew 133% and unsecured loans grew 63% year-over-year, reflecting portfolio diversification 

- Secured and unsecured loans now account for nearly 35% of total balances, up from 27% a year ago 

- Record loan originations of $4.2 billion, up 40% year-over-year, with growth in both secured and unsecured lending 

- Strong performance in SME portfolio and new credit policies enabling safe expansion to lower-risk segments 

### Asset Quality & Risk Metrics

- 15-90 day NPL ratio at 4.2%, performing better than historical Q3 seasonality 

- 90+ day NPL ratio increased marginally to 6.8%, in line with expected seasonality 

- Coverage ratios remain solid despite modest decline, maintaining robust provision buffers 

- Improved recovery levels from reactivated customers who previously defaulted but have since cured their debt 

- Credit portfolio continues to outperform expectations, supported by disciplined underwriting 

### Deposit Growth & Funding

- Deposits reached $38.8 billion, up 34% year-over-year on an FX-neutral basis 

- Cost of funding improved from 91% to 89% of interbank rates despite volume growth 

- Strong deposit inflows across all segments in Brazil, demonstrating depth and resilience 

- Colombia deposits growing steadily with funding costs below interbank rate 

- Mexico experienced some anticipated outflows following deposit rate reductions, aligning with long-term strategy 

### International Expansion (Mexico Focus)

- Mexico operation has reached ~13-14 million customers, representing 14-15% of adult population and 25% of banked Mexicans  

- Mexico customer ARPU of $12.50, approaching Brazil levels despite being a newer market 

- Mexico could be profitable now but management is prioritizing long-term growth over short-term profitability 

- Mexico has a cost-to-serve of ~$1, better than Brazil had at a similar stage of development 

- Loan-to-deposit ratio (LDR) in Mexico is ~15%, indicating significant growth potential 

### AI Strategy & Implementation

- AI integration seen as a "once-in-a-lifetime opportunity" to differentiate from traditional banks 

- Building on years of experience in model governance, privacy, and large-scale model deployment 

- AI implementation helping improve credit decision precision for existing customers 

- Currently applied to credit limit increases but not yet to new customer acquisition, lending, or Mexico/Colombia operations  

- Implementing robust processes to ensure tools promote customer financial well-being with appropriate regulatory guardrails 

### Regulatory Environment & Future Outlook

- Mexico: Concerns about potential caps on interchange fees, actively engaging with industry and government  

- Brazil: No immediate plans to enter mortgage market despite regulatory changes, preferring short-duration credit products  

- FGTS loan originations expected to decrease due to regulatory changes, but not materially affecting overall portfolio 

- Public payroll loans expected to offset potential FGTS headwinds  

- Cautious but optimistic approach to private payroll loans in the medium to long term
