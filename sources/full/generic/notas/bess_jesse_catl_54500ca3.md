# Bess - Jesse - CATL

10 years of experience on ESS, solar,for industrial clients.

CATL on june 2024. Selling systems, DC blocks, to utility scale customers and projects. Sacramento.

What are the main use cases?

8 different applications.

(1) But the two biggest revolve around as energy arbitrage, biggest one.

(2) Utilizing the battery for extreme conditions. Replacing peaker plants, that are fossil fuel plants to dispatch more power.

You have tradings, that arbitrage this price, but you more and more the utilities have to diversify these projects.

These traders freaquently have already power plants.

(3rd) A very important use case, it would add to resilience.

What would be the price for the ESS in terms of ?

100-130 kiwollat hour for a DC block, you should have also rectifiers, inversors to make it AC.

This would go to 150-180 for the full solution in AC.

For the EPC, whole project, 190-200 kwh.

ESS- demand is way up.

Demand for ESS will still be very substantial. We have projects that are going into 2027.

DC blocks - BYD, EV-E. RPT Hithium.

Margins?

Historically - Margins for stationary have been very good. For AC, and DC.

Margins have been comprosseing already.

Our most formidable competitor is Tesla, for a couple of reasons: They are not integrated, but they have early relationship with CATL. Very good at integration and simple solutions with long term warranty. Tesla got some software that help them to forecast and when to sell the power, in terms of arbitrage.

Next most strongest: Sungrow, they have been in the market for a long time.  They are integrated on the Energy Management System, Power Conversion System ( Invertor, Transformer).

FLNC: They dont produce anything themselves, they are not adding a lot on value chain. They are trying to compete on Tesla Side. They are expensive, slow, delivery can be challenging.
