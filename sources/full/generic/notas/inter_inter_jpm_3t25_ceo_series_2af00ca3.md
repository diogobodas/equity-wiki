# Inter - JPM 3T25 Ceo Series

> [bloco não-suportado: transcription]



### Meeting Context

- Call hosted by JPMorgan for clients featuring Inter leadership discussing strategy and financial targets   

- Call focused on Inter's progress toward their "60-30-30" aspirational targets for 2027  

### Inter's Strategic Targets

- "60-30-30" refers to: 60 million clients, 30% ROE, and 30% cost-to-income ratio  

- Currently at approximately 15% ROE and 45% cost-to-income ratio  

- Leadership remains committed to these targets, though timing might extend into 2028  

- Q3 was one of their best quarters for new clients and activation rates 

### Business Model Advantages ("Inter by Design")

- Digital distribution with zero branch costs provides structural advantage  

- Best-in-market cost of funding through retail deposits  

- Focus on collateralized loans rather than unsecured products  

- Portfolio remains approximately 70% collateralized and 30% non-collateralized 

- SME accounts represent 45-50% of deposits despite being only 2.5 million accounts versus 25 million personal accounts 

### Private Payroll Loan Product

- Emerging as a significant growth driver with strong risk-adjusted margins  

- Requires upfront provisioning which impacts short-term earnings, but expected to become profitable soon  

- Serves as customer acquisition channel - 20% of private payroll loans are to new customers 

- Currently provisioning for mid-teens delinquency, but performance trending toward high single-digit rates 

- Offers ROE "north of 30%" with strong cross-selling opportunities  

### Asset Quality Strategy

- Conservative approach to credit underwriting 

- Focus on collateralized products helps manage risk in varying economic conditions  

- Target customer profile is middle-income (5,000-6,000 BRL family income), 35-40 years old  

- Loan portfolio less correlated to macroeconomic shifts due to collateralization  

### SME Banking Opportunity

- Leadership sees SME banking as underserved and overpriced by incumbents  

- Comparable to the opportunity they saw in consumer banking in 2015-17 

- Recent acquisition of Interpag to improve receivables collection solutions 

- More complex than consumer banking but represents significant growth potential  

### Funding and Capital Strategy

- Focus on retail funding at competitive rates  

- Never participated in "fierce competition" of paying above-market rates for deposits 

- Corporate structure designed to optimize capital efficiency and returns  

- Approximately 1.8 billion reais of excess capital held abroad 

### Action Items

- [ ] Leadership to continue monitoring private payroll loan performance as product matures  

- [ ] Team to enhance SME banking capabilities following Interpag acquisition 

- [ ] Management to prepare for potential additional funding request from FGC (Brazilian deposit insurance)
