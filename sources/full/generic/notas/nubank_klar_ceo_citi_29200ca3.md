# Klar CEO - CITI

> [bloco não-suportado: transcription]



### Company Overview

- CLAR generates ~$300 million in run rate revenue with 240 employees, demonstrating high efficiency (operating in Mexico at ~$0.60 on the dollar)

- The company has 2.6-2.7 million active users

- 60% of revenue comes from lending (credit cards, personal loans, secure credit cards)

- They operate as a SOFIPO (regulated entity that takes deposits and lends)

- Current loan book is ~$350-360 million, growing 5-6% month-over-month

- Deposit base is approximately $400 million

### Strategic Initiatives

- Expanding from consumer to B2B segment, aiming to make it 15-20% of their book next year

- Reached agreement to acquire Bineo (a digital bank from Banorte), pending regulatory approvals

- Targeting $500 million in run rate revenue by next year

- Focused on becoming a platform offering multiple products across different segments

- Maintaining discipline on unit economics with payback periods of 12-18 months

### Deposit Strategy

- The "deposit war" in Mexico has moderated, with rates coming down from extreme levels

- CLAR focuses on loan-to-deposit ratio (currently in high 70%), more important than yield

- The company uses fixed-term products to match their short-term loan book (~4-5 months)

- CLAR can target specific deposit amounts needed through promotional campaigns

### Risk Management

- Learned valuable lessons about "second-order risk" (when expanding credit or stacking products)

- Technology gives them advantage in analyzing patterns and dynamically adjusting credit

- Primary risk comes from their own lending decisions, not third-party credit supply

- Carefully manages product stacking and credit line expansions based on user behavior

### Competitive Landscape

- Market has consolidated from ~50 companies to 5-6 well-funded players

- Key competitors include Nubank, Mercado Pago, Revolut (just received approval), and Plata

- Noted that some competitors are pursuing aggressive growth with questionable unit economics

- CLAR acquires 50,000-60,000 credit card users monthly with 12-18 month payback periods

### Regulatory Environment

- Mexico's regulators are currently in "risk-off" mode, less supportive than Brazil's

- Licensing processes are lengthy (Revolut took 5 years to get operational approval)

- Financial system in Mexico still needs to reach the ~40 million adults in the informal economy

### Future Outlook

- IPO could be considered in the future, likely in the US due to investor preferences

- Focus on differentiation and establishing clear market position before IPO discussions

- Company has a "pristine cap table" with sophisticated governance structures

### Action Items

- [ ] Complete regulatory process for Bineo acquisition

- [ ] Expand B2B segment to reach 15-20% of book by next year

- [ ] Maintain disciplined growth with focus on healthy unit economics

- [ ] Monitor competitive landscape, especially new entrants like Revolut and OpenBank
