# Cyrela 3T25

> [bloco não-suportado: transcription]



### Financial Performance

- Cash generation totaled R$423 million for the quarter, including a non-recurring effect of R$251 million from Cury shares sale 

- Recurring operational generation reached R$172 million, higher than Q3 last year

- YTD cash generation totaled R$103 million, 50% below the same period last year 

### Inventory Management

- Inventory grew from R$10 billion at end of last year to R$15 billion in Q3 due to increased launches 

- Stock sales velocity (VSO) has decelerated slightly in the period 

- Ready-to-sell inventory is performing below expectations, while in-progress stock is exceeding targets 

- The composition has shifted to more launch inventory and less ready-to-sell inventory 

### Launch Performance

- Launch volume doubled in the first nine months compared to the same period last year 

- Projects launched in the final weeks of Q3 (mainly Viva São Paulo) showed satisfactory performance in line with expectations 

- A Living project in Rio de Janeiro performed exceptionally well, selling out quickly 

- Management is satisfied with Q4 launch and stock sales performance so far 

### Revenue Recognition

- About 75% of the R$2.1 billion in launch sales had no revenue recognition in Q3, representing approximately R$1.5 billion of off-balance value 

- The corresponding revenue of approximately R$600 million will likely be recognized in Q4 

- Stock sales revenue recognition was the lowest in a long time, contributing to weaker Q3 revenue 

- Epic project surpassed the suspension clause in October and will be recognized in Q4 

### Capital Allocation Strategy

- Management is likely to opt for an extraordinary dividend payment in December 

- The Jardim das Perdizes land acquisition from Tecnisa was canceled 

- Without this acquisition, Q4 should close with less leverage and better cash generation 

- Q4 cash generation is expected to be close to neutral, unlike the significant swings in Q2 and Q3 

- Cash generation for 2026 is expected to be positive, but exact estimates will be provided after planning rounds in December/January 

### VIVAZ Segment Performance

- VIVAZ operation (low-income housing) is performing well with continuously improving metrics 

- Management plans to grow this operation in both São Paulo and Rio de Janeiro 

- Recent changes to the Minha Casa Minha Vida program, including raising the price ceiling from R$264,000 to R$275,000, will positively impact 60-70% of VIVAZ operations in São Paulo 

### Commercial Strategy

- Implementing several strategies to address ready-to-sell inventory challenges:

- Offering more attractive alienation financing conditions to counter higher interest rates 

- Seeking partnerships and alternatives to traditional real estate financing 

- Creating targeted stock campaigns and product packaging 

- Most inventory has been adjusted for inflation (INCC), though ready stock is more challenging to adjust 

### Future Planning

- Low-income segment is expected to gain more space in the company's portfolio for 2026 

- The cancellation of Jardim das Perdizes acquisition may impact the launch pipeline for 2026 

- Management is preparing for the World Cup and elections in the second half of 2026, but doesn't expect major strategy changes
