# Tenda - CFO 19/11

### Business Segments & Performance

- Discussion of company's strategy in Minas Gerais and potential opportunities there. 

- The Tender segment has shown strong sequential improvement with high returns, one of the highest among publicly-traded competitors. 

- Aleia/Leia segment has been struggling with negative margins (-3.8%), but new processes being implemented are expected to help improve performance.  

- Current quarter (Q4) described as "the worst moment" for Leia due to inefficiencies of managing both old works and implementing verticalization simultaneously. 

- Management expects Leia to follow Tenda's improvement trajectory, though it will take time as "this segment doesn't have quick improvements." 

### Construction Methodology

- The company is transitioning to a more verticalized construction model for greater efficiency.  

- Currently only 15-20% of operations use the new verticalized process, but the goal is to reach 100%. 

- The verticalized model is expected to achieve 30% margins when fully implemented. 

- Current challenges include training workers and managing the learning curve for the new methodology.  

- The company established a "PCP" (Planning, Control and Constructive Process) system to ensure sequencing without intervals between works.  

### Geographic Strategy & Market Segments

- São Paulo presents challenges due to its construction technique requirements, with structural vernalism now allowing 11x constructive potential relative to land area. 

- The company is expanding operations in the Northeast region, João Pessoa, and returning to Curitiba after changes in local regulations.  

- Management is diversifying into Faixa 3 (F3) housing segment across Brazil, which has better margins than lower segments.  

- Strategy aims for a balanced distribution across three price segments (likely F1, F2, F3), targeting approximately one-third each.  

- The company is focusing on large land developments, including a 4,000-unit project in Jaú that's ready to launch.  

### Financial Outlook

- Target of reaching 30,000 units, with growth primarily from Rio and São Paulo markets. 

- For next year, projecting 26,000 units (7.5% growth in units, plus 5% price inflation and 5% mix improvement). 

- Expected overall growth of 15% in Gross Sales Value (BGV). 

- Leia segment expected to release the same volume next year as this year (4,000 more units). 

- Cash flow for next year is projected to be "almost neutral" after accounting for share repurchases and dividends.  

### Margin Discussion

- Current margin gap versus competitors (approximately 4 percentage points) attributed to accounting differences, market segment mix, and higher financial costs.  

- Financial expenses represent a significant burden (approximately R$200-300 million annually, or 5% of revenue).  

- Expectations for margin improvement beginning next year, with significant improvement by 2027 as interest rates decline and cash generation improves.  

- PDD (loan loss provisions) currently running at around 2.2% versus the expected 3%. 

### Action Items

- [ ] Return to Curitiba market with new launches following changes in local regulations. 

- [ ] Implement verticalized construction methodology across more projects to improve margins. 

- [ ] Launch project in Jaú that has approximately 4,000 units. 

- [ ] Present to the council next week regarding the Netherlands book. 

- [ ] Expand into F3 (Faixa 3) housing segment across all operational areas. 

- [ ] Consider paying dividends if the Chamber or Senate approves purchases in the PL by March 2016.
