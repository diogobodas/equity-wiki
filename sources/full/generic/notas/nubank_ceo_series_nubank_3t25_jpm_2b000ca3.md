# Ceo Series - Nubank 3T25 JPM

> [bloco não-suportado: transcription]



### Financial Performance Highlights

- Risk-adjusted margins were the main positive surprise of the quarter, with Lago noting they've expanded even as the company moves toward a less risky portfolio   

- Net interest margins (NIMs) saw some contraction due to mix shift toward less risky customers and products 

- The expansion in risk-adjusted NIMs occurred as the drop in NIMs was more than offset by improved credit performance 

- Lago emphasized their conservative approach to provisioning, booking 100% upfront credit provisions for unsecured lending  

### Asset Quality and Credit Strategy

- Asset quality is performing well in both Brazil and Mexico, either in line with or better than expectations 

- Nubank's credit underwriting philosophy remains consistent: they provision for resilience across economic cycles, with every unsecured credit cohort able to withstand losses of 2x and still be NPV positive  

- The company's short-duration credit portfolio (credit cards average 6-7 weeks, unsecured personal loans 5-7 months) provides flexibility to adjust quickly to changing conditions  

- Lago pointed to their advantage in capturing customer behavior data through payments and other interactions beyond just credit products 

### Mexico Growth Strategy

- Mexico's financial penetration today is lower than Brazil's was six years ago across banking, digital payments, and credit card adoption 

- Nubank Mexico products currently have higher ROAs and ROEs than Brazil, though not "massively higher" 

- The company is focusing on the "banked mass market" segment in Mexico, with plans to gradually incorporate unbanked customers  

- Substantial portion of Nubank's Mexico customers are joining the formal banking sector for the first time, with 30-40% of new credit lines going to customers who didn't have previous credit  

- In Mexico, lending is expected to outpace credit card growth, potentially becoming the larger product segment  

### SME Banking Opportunity

- SME segment is currently Nubank's best-performing customer segment across growth, activity, principality, and unit economics 

- Approximately 25% of SMEs in Brazil are already Nubank customers, with an estimated one-third of newly formed SMEs choosing Nubank  

- The SME segment is showing higher NPS than competitors and promising performance data for credit products  

- More than 40% of SME customers are "MEIs" (micro-entrepreneurs in Brazil), with growth now progressing into small and medium businesses  

- Nubank targets a minimum ROE of 30% for the SME segment 

### Middle and Higher-Income Strategy

- Nubank defines middle-income customers as those earning 5,000-12,000 reais per month, labeled internally as "SuperCore" 

- Middle-income segment represents a natural evolution toward more affluent segments, with significant credit product demand  

- This segment is expected to grow faster than the high-income segment, as Nubank's ability to win is higher in the middle-income category 

### Payroll Loans and FGTS

- FGTS (government-backed loans tied to workers' guarantee fund) has been successful but faces headwinds from new regulations starting October 2025 that will reduce monthly originations by more than 50%   

- Public payroll loans (mainly SIAPI) have been growing well, with expansion into state and municipal entities like São Paulo  

- For private payroll loans, Lago expressed caution about the collateral mechanisms, noting first cohorts have shown high payment defaults across the industry   

- Nubank remains "fairly intrigued" but cautious about private payroll, with the main concern being how well the collateral functions work 

### Deposit Strategy and Funding

- Lago acknowledged Nubank is "still less sophisticated than a bank" in deposit design and pricing compared to their asset side capabilities 

- Deposit strategy optimization involves balancing three factors: customer growth/engagement, unit economics, and funding/balance sheet resilience  

- Mexico deposits saw a 10% decline in Q3 due to repricing, but are now seeing net inflows  

- Cost of funding in Mexico is expected to continue improving (decreasing), while Brazil may see flat or slightly higher funding costs in coming quarters 

### Capital Allocation

- No plans for share repurchases or dividend payments within the next 2-3 years 

- Excess capital will be deployed toward growth opportunities in a staged approach, with continued investment contingent on proof points of profitability  

- Lago stated they have "no dogma against dividends or share repurchases," but current investment opportunities are prioritized  

### Action Items

- [] Nubank to continue monitoring private payroll loan collateral performance to determine if/when to accelerate growth in this product  

- [] Nubank to pursue growth opportunities in the SME segment given strong performance metrics  

- [] Develop more sophisticated deposit pricing capabilities to optimize across customer engagement, unit economics and funding resilience
