# Call Stone - 4T25

### Financial Highlights

Full Year 2025 Performance

- Adjusted net income reached R$2.4 billion, with adjusted basic EPS of R$9.71 per share representing 34% year-over-year growth and exceeding guidance of R$9.60  

- When factoring in R$1.8 billion in tax credits executed in H2, adjusted net income would have reached R$6.379 billion, slightly above guidance of R$6.375 billion 

- Successfully distributed R$3 billion in excess capital to shareholders throughout the year, representing a 15% yield 

- Consolidated ROE expanded to 26%, up 6 percentage points year-over-year, reflecting improvements in profitability and capital efficiency 

Q4 2025 Results

- Adjusted net income increased 10% year-over-year, driven by 12% growth in continuing operations 

- Adjusted basic EPS reached R$2.87, up 27% year-over-year, benefiting from net income growth and share repurchases 

- Total revenue and income from continuing operations grew 13% year-over-year to R$3.7 billion, reflecting mid single-digit TPV growth and disciplined pricing 

- Adjusted gross profit from continuing operations grew 9% year-over-year to R$1.7 billion 

- Effective tax rate decreased to 10.3% from 13.7% in Q4 2024, driven by higher benefits from Lado Bank 

### 2026 and 2027 Guidance

2026 Guidance

- Adjusted gross profit expected between R$6.6 billion and R$7.0 billion 

- Adjusted basic EPS projected between R$10.8 and R$11.4 per share 

- Guidance includes the R$2 billion capital distribution through buybacks but excludes Links proceeds  

- Assumes Selic rate at low 12% by end of 2026 

- Mid single-digit TPV growth expected for the year 

2027 Guidance

- Adjusted gross profit expected between R$7.2 billion and R$8.3 billion 

- Adjusted basic EPS projected between R$11.8 and R$13.4 per share 

- Does not include any additional capital distribution beyond 2026 

- Assumes effective tax rate in mid-teens range and Selic at high 11%  

- Operational KPI guidance discontinued as the company is ahead on credit/deposits but behind on TPV relative to original plan  

### Capital Allocation

Ordinary Capital Distribution

- Board approved R$2 billion in excess capital for distribution via share repurchases during 2026 

- Excess capital calculation methodology refined to better align with Brazilian Central Bank standards, adjusting core equity ratio hurdle from 20% to 17% 

Links Divestment

- Received proceeds from Links sale on February 27, releasing slightly over R$3 billion in capital to be returned to shareholders in 2026 

- Board meeting expected in April to approve this specific distribution 

- Combined impact of Links divestment and 2025/2026 buybacks affects guidance by over R$2 billion in gross profit and R$1.3 billion in net income 

Capital Position

- Adjusted net cash position closed Q4 at R$2.6 billion, down R$930 million sequentially due to R$1.3 billion in share repurchases 

- Excluding buybacks, adjusted net cash would have increased by nearly R$350 million 

### Business Operations

Payment Operations

- Client base reached 4.7 million, up 15% year-over-year 

- Heavy user penetration increased to 41% from 38% in prior quarter, reinforcing strategy of deepening engagement 

- MSMB TPV growth decelerated to 5.3% year-over-year driven by challenging macro environment, mix shift toward digital merchants (where the company has less exposure), and higher churn with softer client additions   

- Q1 2026 TPV growth expected to be roughly flat year-over-year, with stronger second half as bundling and cross-sell initiatives gain traction  

Banking Operations

- Active banking clients reached 3.7 million, up 21% year-over-year 

- Client deposits grew 27% year-over-year and 23% quarter-over-quarter to R$11.1 billion 

- Deposit penetration over MSMB TPV increased to 8.2% from 7.1% in prior quarter, demonstrating strong banking strategy execution 

- Time deposits represented 86% of total deposits (up from 84% in prior quarter), reflecting higher adoption of investment products and cash-sweep strategy  

Credit Operations

- Total portfolio reached R$2.8 billion, growing 23% sequentially 

- Merchant solutions (primarily working capital) totaled R$2.5 billion, up 23% quarter-over-quarter 

- Credit card portfolio reached R$300 million, growing 30% sequentially from smaller base 

- Credit revenues reached R$238 million (up 33% sequentially) while provisions totaled R$110 million (up 27%) 

- NPL 15-90 days increased to 4.43%, primarily from payment delays among higher ticket specialized desk clients 

- NPLs above 90 days at 5.21% vs. 5.03% prior quarter, consistent with normal portfolio seasoning 

- Coverage ratio stable at 264% with cost of risk approximately 17% 

- Average monthly credit yield improved to 3.1% from 2.9% in Q3 2025 

### Strategic Priorities

Three Key Focus Areas

1. Payments Excellence: Shifting focus from pure client acquisition to deepening relationships through better engagement, cross-sell, and retention at scale 

2. Banking and Credit Acceleration: Building on solid three-year foundation to accelerate execution while maintaining risk discipline 

3. Operational Efficiency: Leveraging AI transformation to drive productivity gains across the organization 

Competitive Environment

- Market remains rational from pricing standpoint with no irrational growth-at-any-cost behavior observed 

- Some players expanding offerings and strengthening sales footprint, which is natural industry movement 

- Company's challenges more related to internal execution than external competitive pressure 

AI and Technology Initiatives

- Meaningful acceleration in AI applications over past six months  

- Deliberate approach avoiding disconnected pilots, focusing on core operations redesign in customer service, sales, hubs, and risk  

- Successfully implemented AI agents for first-level customer service interactions with significant efficiency gains 

- Democratizing AI tools across organization while centrally monitoring usage and establishing best practices 

- Long-term expectation of massive productivity improvements, though specific impacts too early to quantify in 2027 guidance   

Product and Market Positioning

- Major effort planned to reposition company perception from POS provider to comprehensive financial services partner 

- Selling and marketing expenses will continue growing in 2026 to support this repositioning  

- Banking license not a constraint on product roadmap - full feature development proceeding with existing license 

- Launched PaymentsLink product for MSMBs selling through WhatsApp 

### Operational Dynamics

Expense Management

- Cost of services increased 23% year-over-year (200 bps as % of revenues) driven by higher credit provisions from portfolio growth 

- Financial expenses up 12% but down 30 bps as % of revenues due to low-cost demand deposits offsetting higher CDI rates 

- Admin expenses increased 12% (slight decrease as % of revenues) reflecting ongoing support function leverage 

- Selling expenses up 16% (40 bps increase as % of revenues) due to more even distribution in 2025 vs. 2024's H1-weighted spending 

- Q4 selling expenses impacted by higher turnover in Q3 requiring increased hiring in Q4 

Credit Portfolio Details

- Operating two distinct working capital models: fully digital for smaller merchants (granular/diversified) and analytical desk-based for large SMBs (higher tickets/concentrated) 

- Working capital product maturing well toward expected losses with opportunity to increase margins 

- Credit card product showing uptick but still significant opportunity for growth in 2026 

- Longer-dated products planned for launch in 2026 to support specialized desk 

Macro and Market Headwinds

- Challenging macro environment weighing on smaller clients since Q3 2024 

- Mix shift toward digital merchants (better performing) vs. brick and mortar (company's focus) creating temporary headwind 

- Results demonstrate model resilience in difficult environment with continued competitive pressure
